Las Meninas, Trapped

Originally written in 2018

Mami, ¿yo soy guapa?
Mami, ¿yo soy guapa?

“The centre of the group is occupied by the little Infanta, with her flared pink and gray dress” (Foucault, 13)

Mi pelo rubio
Mi cara guapa

“she is a princess, but at the same time a little girl; she is most marvelously self-possessed in bearing, but is herself possessed by the court and by the royal lineage” (Alpers, 39)

Meninas feas
Mi cara guapa

“Like a donor in prayer, like an angel greeting the Virgin, a maid of honour on her knees is stretching out her hands towards the princess….A little to the right, there stands another maid of honour, also turned towards the Infanta, leaning slightly over her” (F, 13)

Quiero salir en un cuadro de Velazquez
Mami, ¿por qué no me pinta a mi Velazquez?
¿Por qué siempre salís tú y el papa?

“the royal couple, who are the spectacle itself” (F, 16)

Señorita Margarita, yo solo cumplo órdenes
Pero escuche un secreto yo la estoy pintando a usted

“how could we fail to see that invisibility, there in front of our eyes, since it has its own perceptible equivalent, its sealed-in figure, in the painting itself?” (F, 4)

¿Viste que en el cuadro se está pintando un cuadro?
¡Pues en realidad señora yo la estoy pintando A USTED!

“A mere confrontation, eyes catching one another’s glance, direct looks superimposing themselves upon one another as they cross. And yet this slender line of reciprocal visibility embraces a whole complex network of uncertainties, exchanges, and feints” (F, 5).

¿¡¡¡CÓMOOOO!!!?

“the spectator and the model, reverse their roles infinity” (F, 5)

ESTOY HACIENDO ALGO REVOLUCIONARIO
LLAMAME VELAZQUEZ AKA EXTRAORDINARIO
EL NIÑO DE SEVILLA VINO A SUBIR EL NIVEL
A ENSEÑARLE AL TONTO CÓMO SE TIENE QUE HACER

“Velázquez sees himself as part of the very court he sees through” (A, 40)

¿Entonces yo soy guapa?

“The entire picture is looking out at a scene for which it is itself a scene” (F, 15)

Mm.. sí

“That territory of the 'unsaid,' the impossible to say, to verbalize, is the privileged place that Las Meninas so amazingly exemplifies under the appearance of a simple, everyday, informal, 'frozen' family portrait” (de Diego, 167)

Es que no dejas hablar, no dejas hablar
A ver, es nuestro trabajo, es nuestro trabajo
Margarita, déjame hablar

“the world seen is before us because we… are what commanded its presence” (A, 38)

Margarita, escucha, un momento, por favor

“A shock that is augmented and made more inevitable still by a marginal trap” (F, 6)


Works Cited
Alpers, Svetlana. 1983. "Interpretation without Representation, or, the Viewing of Las Meninas." Representations 1: 30-42.

de Diego, Estrella. 2003. “Representing Representation: Reading Las Meninas, Again.” In Veláquez’s Las Meninas, edited by Suzanne L. Stratton-Pruitt, 150-169. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.

Foucault, Michel. [1966] 2002. The Order of Things: An Archeology of the Human Sciences. London: Routledge.
