Cien Años

Cien años, 2020

The shared alienation of enclosed life during 2020 was reminiscent of the predicament at the center of Café Tacvba's 1994 story-song "El Metro." 

The song's narrator describes being stuck in the Mexico City subway system for months, amassing street wares from vendors and longing for their partner. The narrator's hair and wrinkles are growing, they're losing their sense of time, and all they want is a kiss from their partner who they fear has already forgotten about them. "El Metro" includes a short, bouncy melody on keyboard that repeats throughout and alludes to the Super Mario Bros. theme music. Café Tacvba reminds us of the familiar video game tune to express how riding the Mexico City subway system can feel at times like being on a lonely and whirlwind adventure in an underworld.

This poster / collage came about in the summer of 2020 when in the early months of the pandemic many of us agreed to take the challenge of what felt like 100 years of solitude. But then it was decided that those individuals acts of collective caregiving were too much. The effort to avoid peril was abandoned. Game over!
